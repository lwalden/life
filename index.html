Conway's Game of Life

Cycle: 0

Rules of the Game

  • Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies (underpopulation).
  • Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on.
  • Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies (overpopulation).
  • Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes alive (reproduction).

The board contains 6,400 cells. Cells on the edge wrap around to the opposite side (toroidal grid), allowing gliders and similar patterns to traverse the entire board continuously.
